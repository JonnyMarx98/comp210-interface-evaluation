Introduction

Kuthwala is a 3D puzzle game made in Unreal Engine 4

Kuthwala is the newest game from Toolbox Studios, featuring an expansive open world environment, with challenging puzzles dotted along the path to the final goal, the Temple! To add another layer of challenge and variety to our game, the player can swap between two different animals, each with unique abilities, which come in handy when tackling the plethora of puzzles scattered around the world!


On this website I will evaluate the usability of Kuthwala, particularly the control scheme, difficulty and intuativeness.

Methods

Qualitative Data

To collect qualitative data I asked five people to play through the first 4 puzzles of Kuthwala.  After they had finished I interviewed them to obtain some detailed qualitative data. All 5 of the interviewees are computing for games students so this can also count as a heuristic evaluation. Below are the questions I used.

Quantitative Data

To collect quantitative data I asked 20 people to play the same few puzzles as the people who were interviewed. I also timed how long it took for the each of the 5 interviewees to complete the first 4 puzzles to provide some more quantitative data and find more correlations.

Observation

To collect observational data, firstly I observed the 5 interviewees while they played through the first 4 puzzles and made some notes for each one. I also recorded the screen of 5 people who completed the questionnaire to use as observational data. I will watch and analyse these recordings and discuss any trends I notice in the discussion section.

Discussion

From the interview/heuristic evaluation quite a few problems were highlighted. One problem was that there was no tooltip for how to sprint, however only 2 participants mentioned this. The majority found the tooltips helpful but one participant who said they were helpful also suggested having something to indicate where the tooltips are . Another thing I noticed was that 4 out of 5 said that they found themselves using the panther more than the bear, mainly because it was much faster. This shows that there isn't a good balance between the two characters, slowing the panther could potenionally even them out a bit more.
